# Clarity Agent

*An AI that helps you think before you build — asking the hard questions, making sure you're ready
for whatever might go wrong, and distilling your ideas and your reasoning for you and everyone who
comes after: teammates, stakeholders, and AI agents alike.*

**The problem:** Developing clarity about what you want is the hard part of any creative endeavor,
and it's hard to do it alone -- you don't just need to build, look at what you've made, think over
what you want, and iterate. You also need someone to ask you hard questions, to come up with the
corner cases that will stretch your thinking, to help you realize and plan for what might go wrong
*before* it happens. There are ways to do all of these things, but they can require a lifetime of
expertise and a room full of colleagues. What if your AI could make you wiser and smarter, instead
of just faster?

**The solution:** The Clarity Agent brings together decades of expertise in everything from product
design to failure analysis, and builds it into your AI so that when you sit down and say "hey, I
want to do this" it doesn't just rush off and start building -- it knows when to stop and ask
questions, when to help you figure out what you really want. It's the beginning of a broader plan to
make AI genuinely helpful by helping people get to the goals they actually care about, whether they
can say them crisply or not.

The agent consists of four layers:

1. An *intellectual corpus* of methods and ideas to use in all stages of the life of a project;
2. An *AI-facing encoding* of these in formats useful for AIs in different situations (consumer AI
   chat, coding agents, and so on);
3. *Infrastructure* for the more sophisticated environments to manage the life of a project plan and
   let the AI guide you and capture knowledge effectively; and
4. *User experiences* through which you can access this: a dedicated web app, coding agent
   integrations, and future surfaces like integration with general-purpose AI tools.

While the concepts are generic, we've decided to focus the first version of Clarity Agent on helping
with software-shaped problems, just to give us a concrete place to start.
