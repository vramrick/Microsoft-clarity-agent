# Problem

Police accountability march and rally in Oakland, responding to OPD's fatal shooting of a 24-year-old Black man during a traffic stop two weeks ago. Body cam footage is disputed; the DA is slow-walking the case.

**Event:** Permitted. Frank Ogawa Plaza at 2pm. 1.5-mile route to OPD HQ and back. Stage and sound permit at the plaza; no stage at the endpoint. Expected turnout: 500–1,500.

**Organizer:** Experienced. 8 confirmed marshals (targeting 15 by Saturday; 5 are known quantities). No separate de-escalation team — marshals handle it. OPD liaison contact; civil but not warm relationship.

**Counter-protest:** 20–30 people based on social intel. Known group, loud, may try to infiltrate the route, no violence history but disorganized — which makes them unpredictable.

**Media:** Relationships with Tribune and local TV assignment desks. Draft advisory in progress. Goal: keep the story on the march and the family, not the counter-protesters.

**Success:** The event proceeds safely, the story is the family and accountability, and the counter-protest doesn't shape the narrative.

## What needs to be figured out in the next two days

1. **Counter-protest protocol** — Most urgent. If it goes sideways on camera, it shapes everything.
2. **Speaker order** — 7 confirmed, need to cut to 5 and sequence them for maximum impact.
3. **Media advisory** — Draft exists, needs finalization.
4. **Marshal coverage** — Route coverage with 8–15 marshals.
