# Problem

Tyler Brooks is a career-changer transitioning from 6 years in the USMC (2018–2024) into software engineering. He is mid-way through a B.S. in Computer Science (ACC, expected 2026) and has self-taught Python, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, Git, and Linux.

After 10 weeks and ~80 applications, he has 3 recruiter screens, no technical interviews, no offers.

## What Tyler actually has (mostly invisible on his current resume)

**Security Force Assistance Team, Afghanistan (2018–2022, incl. 2 tours to Kandahar):**
- Embedded advisor to Afghan National Army units — cross-cultural coordination, building working relationships with foreign counterparts, managing schedules and logistics across units with poor organic communication
- Conversationally fluent Pashto, intermediate reading — a rare credential

**Training NCO, School of Infantry (2022–2024):**
- Ran the 12-week small-unit tactics course curriculum
- Managed a section of 4 instructors
- Designed 3 new training modules; 2 are still in use (demonstrable lasting impact)

**Rank at separation: E-6 Staff Sergeant, Honorable Discharge**

**Technical/coding:** Python (basic to-do app, small scripts), JavaScript, HTML/CSS, Git, Linux. No substantial projects yet.

## The actual diagnosis

The resume fails on two levels:
1. **Missing content:** 6 years of service is a single line. Leadership, cross-org coordination, curriculum design, lasting institutional impact — none of it is visible.
2. **Possible targeting mismatch:** Tyler's background (SFAT, Pashto, likely clearance) may make him unusually competitive in defense tech / government contracting, but he may be applying to generic SWE roles where that background doesn't stand out.

## What success looks like

Consistent progression to technical interviews and ultimately an entry-level SWE offer — likely within 4–8 weeks of having a strong resume and targeting the right roles.
