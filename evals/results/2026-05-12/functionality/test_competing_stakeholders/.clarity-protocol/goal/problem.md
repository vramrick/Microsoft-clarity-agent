# Problem Statement

A Director of Engineering is caught between a Sales commitment and Engineering capacity. The VP of Sales committed to a $1.2M enterprise expansion deal contingent on shipping SSO (SAML) + user provisioning (SCIM, fine-grained roles, audit logging) in Q2. The engineering lead estimates Q3 at earliest. The team is mid-stream on a platform migration that was prioritized last quarter. The Director was not in the room when the commitment was made and has been unable to resolve the conflict in three days.

The core tension: a real revenue commitment vs. a realistic delivery estimate, with a shared dependency (the platform migration) complicating any acceleration path.

## Why This Matters

- $1.2M deal at risk if the commitment isn't honored or creatively renegotiated
- Engineering team morale and platform migration at risk if they're forced to context-switch
- The Director's credibility is on the line with both sides
- Pattern risk: if Sales can make commitments without Engineering input, this will happen again

## Success Criteria

- A clear path forward that the Director can present to both Sales and Engineering this week
- Either: a credible plan to hit Q2, or a negotiated alternative the customer will accept
- Clarity on how to prevent this class of problem in the future
