# Problem Statement

Choosing the right entry path into a data science career. The decision has already been made to pivot — the question is which of three options makes most sense given personal circumstances: (A) 12-week full-time bootcamp ($15k, requires quitting current job), (B) 9-month part-time bootcamp ($9k, done while working), or (C) self-study via Coursera specializations. Background includes a completed Coursera ML course, basic Python, and ~1 year of serious research into the field. Context: 10 years in marketing/B2B SaaS, two young kids (5 and 2), mortgage, spouse works as accountant (second income exists but finite). Learns well with structure and accountability; less confident in fully self-directed study.

## Why This Matters

This is a high-stakes, largely irreversible decision in the near term. The wrong path could mean $15k spent and months lost, or a slower transition that stalls momentum. Getting it right means landing a DS role that's well-matched to skills and timeline.

## Success Criteria

- A clear recommendation (or ranking) among the three options with reasoning grounded in personal situation
- A realistic transition timeline
- Confidence that the chosen path actually leads to employable DS skills
