# Requirements

## Financial constraints
The chosen path must not require quitting current job. The effective cost of the full-time bootcamp ($15k tuition + ~$32k lost income) makes it financially untenable given mortgage and two dependents. Part-time bootcamp ($9k, income preserved) is the viable format.

## Structure and accountability
Self-study is too high a drift risk. The program must have structured deadlines, a defined curriculum, and external accountability — not a self-paced menu. The ML Coursera course succeeded precisely because of this; the entry path must replicate it.

## Program quality signals (must verify before enrolling)
- CIRR membership (cirr.org) — non-negotiable filter; unaudited outcomes are marketing
- LinkedIn alumni audit: graduates 12–24 months out holding DS or analytics roles at recognizable companies, with job descriptions that reference modeling/ML work
- Curriculum includes model deployment, git/reproducible code, real capstone projects, SQL beyond basics
- School will answer directly: what % of graduates land modeling roles vs. analyst/BI roles?

## Honest first-role expectations
Target roles that leverage marketing domain expertise: marketing data scientist, growth analyst, revenue analytics. Expected year-one salary $80–95k (near parity). Generic junior analyst roles ($65–75k) are a fallback, not the target. "Data Scientist" title at year one is possible but competitive; don't plan around it.

## Pre-enrollment requirements
1. Complete 3–5 informational conversations with working data scientists in B2B SaaS (reality-check on day-to-day work, what they look for in bootcamp hires)
2. Have the explicit spouse conversation — name the specific ask (3 evenings/week, 9 months) and get genuine agreement, not vibe-level support
3. Agree on a protected study schedule before enrolling, not after

## Timeline
~14–16 months from decision to first day at new job is realistic. Salary parity with current $95k likely in years 2–3 if landing domain-adjacent DS/analytics role; years 3–4 for generic analyst path.
