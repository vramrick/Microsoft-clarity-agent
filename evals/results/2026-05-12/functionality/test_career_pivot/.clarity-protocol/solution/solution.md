# Solution

## Recommendation

**Part-time bootcamp (9 months, ~$9k), not the full-time bootcamp and not self-study.**

Self-study is ruled out by learning style — this person needs external structure and accountability. The full-time bootcamp is ruled out by effective cost: $15k tuition plus ~$32k in lost income at current salary = ~$47k total impact, untenable with two dependents and a mortgage. The part-time bootcamp preserves income, provides the structure needed, and costs $9k out of pocket.

## The Plan

### Step 0 — Talk to working data scientists first (before enrolling anywhere)
Talk to 3–5 DS practitioners in B2B SaaS or adjacent industries, specifically people 2–4 years into the role. Find them via LinkedIn cold outreach — short, honest message explaining the situation gets ~20–30% response rate.

Ask them:
- What does a typical week look like, task by task?
- What skills do you use daily that your training underemphasized?
- What surprised you about the job versus what you expected?
- If you've hired a bootcamp grad, what made you say yes or no?

Prioritize alumni from programs under consideration — one conversation covers both research threads.

### Step 1 — Evaluate and select a program

Starting shortlist: Springboard (Data Science track), General Assembly (part-time DS), Flatiron School DS program. Evaluate each against:

1. **CIRR membership** — check cirr.org first. Non-CIRR outcomes are self-reported.
2. **LinkedIn alumni audit** — graduates from 12–24 months ago. Check titles, companies, and whether job descriptions reference modeling work vs. dashboards/reporting. Note "dark alumni" (graduated, no DS role visible) as a warning signal.
3. **Curriculum checklist** — must include model deployment, git/reproducible code, real capstone projects, SQL depth.
4. **Direct question to the school** — what % of graduates land modeling roles vs. analyst/BI roles?

### Step 2 — Have the real spouse conversation

Frame it concretely: "This means I'm unavailable 3 evenings a week for 9 months. Solo bedtime on those nights. We're spending $9k and I'll probably earn $10–20k less for the first year or two after I switch. The long-term bet is I'm doing work I want to do and earning more by year 3–4. Do you actually agree to that?"

Get agreement on a specific schedule before enrolling. Couples who struggle with this usually didn't have this conversation — they had the vibes version.

### Step 3 — Enroll and protect the time

Treat study time as a work commitment. The schedule should be agreed-on with the spouse and non-negotiable in the same way a work meeting is.

### Timeline

| Phase | Duration |
|---|---|
| Informational conversations + program research | 4–6 weeks |
| Spouse conversation + enrollment decision | 1–2 weeks |
| Bootcamp (while working) | 9 months |
| Portfolio building (starts ~month 6 of bootcamp) | Overlapping |
| Active job search | 2–4 months |
| **First day at new job** | **~14–16 months total** |

## Target roles

Lead with domain expertise. Target: marketing data scientist, growth analyst, revenue analytics — these value the B2B SaaS marketing background and typically pay $80–95k, near current salary parity. Avoid applying broadly for anything with "data" in the title; generic junior analyst roles are the fallback, not the plan.

## What success looks like

Landing a role in year 1 that involves modeling work (not just dashboards), at a salary close to current ($80–95k), in a domain where the marketing background is an asset. Reaching salary parity or above by year 3, with actual advancement trajectory into more technical work.
