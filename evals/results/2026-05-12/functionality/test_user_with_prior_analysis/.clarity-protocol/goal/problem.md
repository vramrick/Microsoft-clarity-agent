# Problem

A 30-engineer org (3 teams) has outgrown its current structure. Platform is a bottleneck — Growth and Data both depend on it, and priority conflicts are constant. Growth contains two structurally distinct workstreams (acquisition surfaces and retention/monetization) that share a manager but not a stack. Data is under-led for its size; the EM (Lina) joined 4 months ago and is still ramping.

**Current state:** Platform (8, Maya — senior engineer), Growth (12, Darius — senior engineer), Data (10, Lina — EM, 4 months in). All report to the eng director.

**Success looks like:** Bottleneck is resolved with clear ownership, workstreams have focused leadership, Data has a sustainable path to director-level leadership, and the director's span remains manageable.

**Prior work:** 12 skip-level 1:1s, three proposal drafts, informal CTO read-out (supportive, wants pressure-testing). Matrix, functional split, and "fix it with process" have been considered and rejected.

## Proposal

- Split Growth → Acquisition (6) and Retention (6), each with a TL promoted from within
- Split Platform → Foundations (5) and Developer Experience (3). Foundations reports to director. DX reports to Maya. Maya's title shifts to "principal engineer with a small team."
- Add an EM under Lina on Data to give her a runway toward director

Net result: 3 teams → 5, direct reports: 3 → 5.

## Open Questions for Pressure-Testing

1. Does the Foundations/DX split actually resolve the bottleneck, or does it just move the conflict?
2. Will Maya read the reframing as a demotion?
3. What risks has the director not yet identified?
4. How should comms be sequenced to leads and the broader org?
