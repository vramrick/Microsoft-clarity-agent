# Problem Statement

Logo churn at this B2B SaaS company has risen from ~7% to ~11% annually over the last three quarters — a 57% relative increase that is now material enough to require explanation and a response plan. The Head of Customer Success needs to understand *why* accounts are churning (not just that they are), and present a credible "churn plan" to the COO in approximately six weeks as part of annual planning.

The core problem is that the causes of this churn increase are unknown. Without knowing why, any intervention is a guess. The work is to design and execute a rigorous research program — likely combining churned customer interviews, cohort-level usage analysis, and a survey of at-risk accounts — that produces a defensible diagnosis, then translate that into a plan the COO can act on.

## Why This Matters

Churn at 11% means roughly 1 in 9 customers is leaving each year. If unchecked, this erodes ARR faster than new business can compensate and signals a product-market or value delivery problem. Annual planning is the moment when headcount, tooling, and strategic priorities get set — arriving with a research-backed diagnosis rather than a gut-feel plan creates credibility and ensures the right resources are committed.

**COO's expected output:** Diagnosis + intervention recommendations with resource estimates. She is pragmatic and won't accept interventions without a grounded problem statement, but she also won't sit still for a research report without a "so what." The deliverable is: here's why churn went up, here's what we should do about it, here's what it takes.

**Resourcing:** Primarily the Head of CS, with ~20-30% of one analytics partner (shared with ops). Four CSMs available for warm outreach and synthesizing their own accounts' notes — not for research full time.

## Known Context

**Existing hypotheses (anecdotal, not validated):** Pricing competitiveness against one aggressive mid-market competitor; onboarding struggles — some accounts never got traction post-implementation. Neither has been tested rigorously.

**Churned population:** ~40 accounts over the last 2 quarters. No formal segmentation done yet; off-hand appears to be a mix of sizes and verticals.

**Available data:**
- Product usage: feature adoption, DAU, session depth
- Support ticket history with sentiment tags
- Quarterly NPS (~15% response rate — low coverage)
- CRM renewal/cancellation notes (quality inconsistent by CSM)
- No structured analysis of churned accounts has been done in the last 18 months

## Success Criteria

- Research program designed and scoped with clear methods, questions, and timelines that fit within a ~6-week window
- Findings identify the primary drivers of churn increase (ideally distinguishing structural vs. fixable causes)
- COO presentation tells a clear story: what we found, what we recommend, what it will take
- Plan is credible enough that COO can defend it to the board if needed
