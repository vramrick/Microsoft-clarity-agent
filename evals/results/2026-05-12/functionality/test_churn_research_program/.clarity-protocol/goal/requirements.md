# Requirements

## Must-Haves

**Fits in 6 weeks.** The full program — research, synthesis, and COO presentation — must complete before annual planning. Any method that can't be executed and analyzed within the window is out of scope for this cycle.

**Diagnosis before interventions.** The COO will not accept intervention recommendations without a grounded problem statement. Root causes must be identified and evidenced, not asserted.

**Interventions include resource estimates.** Recommendations must specify who owns them and roughly what they require (headcount, budget, tooling), not just what they are.

**Respects bandwidth constraints.** Analytics partner has ~20-30% capacity. CSMs cannot be pulled from their books for research full-time. The program must be executable within those limits.

**Handles sensitive accounts thoughtfully.** Outreach to accounts that ended badly must not re-litigate incidents or feel extractive to the customer.

## Quality Standards

**Findings must be defensible.** The COO may need to take this to the board. Evidence must be traceable to source.

**Distinguish cause from accelerant.** For incident-related churn, determine whether operational failures caused departures or accelerated ones already forming — these require different interventions.

**Quantitative and qualitative must cross-validate.** Interview themes should be tested against behavioral data; contradictions are findings, not noise.

## Non-Scope

- Winning back churned accounts
- Implementing identified fixes (interventions are scoped and recommended, not executed, in this cycle)
- Designing a recurring survey program
- Root-cause analysis of the service failure incident itself
