# Stakeholders

## Primary

**Head of Customer Success** — owns the research program, designs and runs interviews, synthesizes findings, authors and presents the churn plan. The primary decision-maker on research design and prioritization.

**COO** — the audience for the final deliverable. Pragmatic; wants diagnosis + interventions with resource estimates, not a research report. Will push back on proposals without grounded problem statements. Has ~6 weeks until annual planning.

**Analytics Partner** (shared resource, ~20-30% capacity) — owns Phase 1 quantitative analysis. Needs a clear, scoped brief to commit the sprint. Critical dependency for behavioral fingerprinting and cohort segmentation.

**CSM Team (4 people)** — supporting role: warm outreach to interview candidates, structured review of their churned accounts' notes. Have the customer relationships but carry active books; can't be put on research full-time.

## Secondary

**Churned customers (40 accounts)** — primary research subjects. A subset (~10–12) will be interviewed; all 40 will receive the lightweight survey. Include 2–3 politically sensitive accounts requiring careful handling, one involving a significant service failure.

**Retained at-risk customers** — secondary signal; their behavioral patterns form the comparison cohort for quantitative analysis. Not directly engaged in this research cycle.

**Board** — indirect audience; the COO may need to defend the churn plan at the board level. The plan needs to be credible at that level of scrutiny.

## Adversarial Context

**Aggressive mid-market competitor** — named hypothesis for competitive churn. Understanding their positioning and where they're winning is material to the intervention design, even if they're not directly engaged in the research.
