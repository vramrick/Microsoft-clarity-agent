# Summary

## What We're Building

Chris is building a curated online marketplace connecting freelance writers with editors and publishers — focused on journalism and nonfiction, where quality and professional standards actually mean something. Think of it as the anti-Upwork: you can't just sign up, you have to earn your way in, and once you're in, you're part of a community that takes the work seriously.

The problem it solves is real: serious writers and editors are underserved by generic freelance platforms that treat writing like any other commodity gig. There's no good place for a literary journalist to find an editor who understands long-form narrative, or for a fact-checking-obsessed editor to find writers who share that ethic. This platform builds that place.

## The Plan

The platform gates entry through an application and review process — Chris personally vets every writer and editor against documented criteria. Accepted members build profiles, editors post briefs, writers pitch ideas, and work happens through a structured assignment flow with escrow payments to protect both sides. Ratings and a ranking system build reputation over time, and the best writers rise to the top.

The Year 1 goal is small and deliberate: ~100 writers and ~50 editors, recruited largely from Chris's existing network in journalism and nonfiction. Quality before scale. The platform is built lean — Sharetribe (or equivalent) for the marketplace infrastructure, Stripe for payments, Airtable for Chris's operations workflow — so the concept can be validated before committing to expensive custom development.

Revenue comes from editor subscriptions (tiered by access level) and a transaction fee on each project payment. Writers join free.

## Why This Can Work

The core insight is that trust is the product, not the technology. Writers trust that editors on this platform are real professionals who pay; editors trust that writers have been vetted and can deliver. Both trust the platform because it has skin in the game — it enforces standards, holds payments in escrow, and removes bad actors. That trust is what justifies the subscription fee and the transaction cut, and it's something a generic platform can't easily replicate.

Chris has the right foundation: existing relationships on both sides of the market, deep domain credibility, and a clear-eyed view of what he's trying to build and why.
