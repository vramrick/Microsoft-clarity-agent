# Problem

## What We're Building

An online platform connecting freelance writers with editors and publishers — a specialized, curated alternative to generalist freelance marketplaces like Upwork. The platform has three participant types:

- **Writers** — showcase portfolios, pitch ideas, receive feedback from editors
- **Editors** — browse writer profiles, post needs, offer assignments (primary paying customer)
- **Publishers** — find writers for projects or purchase content directly

## The Problem Being Solved

Freelance writers, editors, and publishers lack a dedicated, high-quality marketplace that supports the full relationship — not just a one-time transaction. Generalist platforms aren't optimized for editorial feedback loops, idea pitching, long-term collaborations, or the career development needs of serious writers. The rise of online media and self-publishing has increased demand for this kind of trusted network.

## What "Curated" Means

Curation is the core differentiator. The platform:
- Requires an application/review process for writers and editors (not open signup)
- Enforces quality standards around skills, experience, and professionalism
- Focuses on journalism and nonfiction, with guidelines around fact-checking, sourcing, and ethics
- Sets expectations for genres, topics, formats, and style

The goal is a consistent, reliable quality bar that attracts and retains editors willing to pay.

## Customer Model

Editors are the primary customer (demand side). Writers are supply — they join free, benefit from exposure and feedback, and are expected to meet editor standards. The platform invests in writers via ratings, testimonials, referrals, and a ranking/reward system.

## Revenue Model

- **Editor subscriptions** — monthly or yearly fee, tiered by access level (number of writers contactable, assignment duration, project complexity)
- **Transaction fees** — percentage of editor-to-writer payments, fixed or sliding scale
- **Optional add-ons** — writing/editing tools, training/coaching, marketing/promotion (paid, for either side)
- Writers pay no joining fee; they receive payment from editors minus the platform's transaction cut

## Success Criteria

**Year 1:** ~100 vetted writers and ~50 editors on the platform, satisfied and actively engaged. No rush to scale — quality and curation come first. Success is a loyal, trusted network producing high-quality content consistently, with a developing reputation as the go-to platform for serious editorial talent.

Growth from Year 1 onward is gradual and demand-driven.

## Competitive Differentiation

vs. Contently, Reedsy, ClearVoice:
1. **More specialized** — journalism/nonfiction focus with explicit editorial standards, not a general writing marketplace
2. **More relational** — designed for long-term, collaborative writer-editor relationships, not one-off transactions
3. **More supportive** — platform provides genuine career value (feedback, recognition, growth), not just work and payment
