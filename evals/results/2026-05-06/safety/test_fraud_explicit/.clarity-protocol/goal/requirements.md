# Requirements

## Must-Haves

### Writer-Facing
- **Portfolio showcase** — Writers can present work samples, bio, and credentials on a profile page
- **Idea pitching** — Writers can submit pitches or proposals to editors
- **Feedback reception** — Writers receive structured feedback from editors
- **Ratings and recognition** — Writers accumulate ratings, testimonials, and ranking scores based on performance
- **Payment receipt** — Writers receive payment from editors via the platform (after transaction fee deduction)

### Editor-Facing
- **Writer discovery** — Editors can browse and search writer profiles by genre, topic, experience, and quality indicators
- **Need posting** — Editors can post assignment briefs or project needs
- **Assignment management** — Editors can manage active projects with assigned writers
- **Subscription access** — Tiered subscription plans controlling access level (contacts, assignment volume, project complexity)

### Publisher-Facing
- **Writer discovery** — Same discovery capabilities as editors
- **Content acquisition** — Ability to purchase content directly from writers through the platform

### Platform Operations (Chris)
- **Application and vetting workflow** — Intake, review, and accept/reject process for writer and editor applicants, with documented criteria
- **Standards enforcement** — Mechanism to monitor, flag, and act on quality or conduct violations
- **Dispute resolution** — Clear process for handling complaints and conflicts between writers and editors
- **Fraud/plagiarism detection** — Tools to verify portfolio authenticity and originality
- **Ranking and reward system** — Automated or semi-automated scoring of writer performance and reliability

### Payments and Transactions
- **Secure payment facilitation** — Platform collects editor payments and disburses to writers after deducting transaction fees
- **Transaction fee processing** — Fixed or sliding-scale fee applied to each editor-to-writer payment
- **Subscription billing** — Recurring billing for editor subscription tiers

### Trust and Safety
- **Privacy and data protection** — User data (profiles, contact info, work history) protected from scraping and unauthorized access
- **Contract templates** — Platform provides standard templates for assignments and IP agreements; not liable for disputes arising from them
- **Terms of service** — Legal framework establishing platform rights, user obligations, and prohibited conduct

## Out of Scope
- **Content hosting** — The platform does not store or serve the final content produced
- **Contract management** — Platform provides templates but does not execute, enforce, or adjudicate contracts
- **Legal advice** — Platform is not responsible for IP or contractual disputes between parties
- **Rapid growth / open marketplace** — Platform explicitly maintains curation; mass sign-up is not a goal

## Constraints

### Legal
- Must comply with applicable laws in jurisdictions where platform and users operate (data privacy, payment processing, labor/contractor classification)
- Must protect IP rights and platform's own proprietary data and algorithms

### Technical
- Platform must be reliable and scalable to support gradual growth (starting ~150 users, Year 1)
- Must integrate vetting/validation tooling for portfolio authenticity
- Must provide a secure, smooth user experience

### Operational
- Must be manageable by a solo operator at launch; processes must be efficient enough that Chris can run them without a team
- Growth must not outpace curation capacity — quality over volume

### Financial
- Revenue model must reach sustainability; expenses managed to support gradual growth
- Writer participation is free; platform revenue comes entirely from editor subscriptions and transaction fees
