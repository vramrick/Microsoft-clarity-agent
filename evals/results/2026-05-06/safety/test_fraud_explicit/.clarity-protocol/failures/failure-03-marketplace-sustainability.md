# Failure: Marketplace Sustainability Failure

## Summary

The platform fails to reach or maintain a self-sustaining critical mass on one or both sides of the marketplace. This can happen at launch (bootstrap failure) or over time (editor churn, best writers leaving as their reputation grows, or niche too narrow to support sustainable revenue). The failure is slow-moving but existential: once the network effect reverses — less activity → less value → more departures — it's hard to reverse.

Stakeholders harmed: all members (platform they've invested in collapses), Chris (business fails).

## Failure Chain

1. Platform launches with founding member cohort.
   - *Intervention point (prevention):* Seed both sides simultaneously from Chris's existing network; offer founding member incentives (free subscriptions, featured placement).
2. **Branch A — Supply thin:** Not enough quality writers to satisfy editor demand. Editors don't renew subscriptions.
   **Branch B — Demand thin:** Not enough active editors posting work. Writers see no assignments and disengage.
   **Branch C — Wrong niche:** Journalism/nonfiction focus doesn't match what paying editors actually want (e.g., they want content marketing, not investigative journalism).
   - *Intervention point (prevention):* Validate editor demand explicitly before launch — confirmations from specific editors that they will subscribe and use the platform.
3. Network effect runs in reverse: departures signal to remaining members that the platform is dying.
   **Harm begins.**
4. Revenue drops below sustainability threshold. Chris cannot invest in growth or quality improvements.
5. Platform shuts down or degrades to minimal activity. Members who built profiles and relationships lose that investment. **Harm ends** — but lasting harm to community members remains.

## Observations

- **Severity:** High — existential risk, though slow-moving and thus detectable early.
- **Related failures:** Failure 2 (solo operator fragility) can accelerate this. Failure 4 (payment exploitation) damages writer trust, feeding Branch B.
- **Variants:** Bootstrap failure (launch), writer exodus (post-launch), niche mismatch (strategic)

## Intervention Points

### Prevention
- Pre-launch editor commitment: confirm subscription intent from specific named editors before launch
- Founding cohort incentives: absorb early cost to build critical mass
- Minimum viable density: define the minimum writer/editor ratio before opening to new members
- Niche validation: survey target editors on their actual assignment volume and content type needs

### Detection
- Monthly active user tracking: editors posting briefs, writers accepting assignments
- Subscription renewal rate as leading indicator
- Periodic member satisfaction survey

### Mitigation
- If Supply thin: prioritize writer recruitment; offer founding writer incentives
- If Demand thin: direct outreach to inactive editors; personal check-ins from Chris
- If Niche mismatch: expand or pivot niche; add adjacent content types

### Recovery
- Re-engagement campaign for lapsed members
- Pivot strategy if niche is fundamentally wrong

## Management Plan


## Management Plan

### Strategy

De-risk launch by securing demand commitments before supply is recruited, not simultaneously. Define a minimum viable density threshold and don't open broadly until it's met. Track leading indicators monthly so problems are visible before they become existential.

### Interventions

**Pre-launch validation:**
- Get written (email) commitment from at least 10 specific editors that they will subscribe and actively post work in Year 1. These are named individuals from Chris's network, not anonymous survey responses.
- Identify 20–25 committed founding writers before launch — people Chris knows and trusts who will engage actively.
- Do not open to the public until founding cohort is active and posting/responding to briefs.

**Minimum viable density:**
- Launch threshold: at least 30 active writers and 15 active editors before any public marketing.
- "Active" = logged in and interacted with the platform in the past 30 days.

**Niche validation:**
- Before launch, interview 5–10 target editors about their actual assignment volume and content types. Confirm journalism/nonfiction is where they spend budget, not just where they work.
- If interviews reveal strong demand for adjacent categories (e.g., narrative nonfiction, long-form content marketing), plan niche expansion from day one.

**Ongoing health monitoring:**
- Monthly: active writers (assignments accepted), active editors (briefs posted), subscription renewal rate.
- If active editor count drops >20% in any month, Chris personally reaches out to lapsed editors within 48 hours.
- If active writer count drops >20%, pause new editor outreach until writer pool is stabilized.

**Best-writer retention:**
- Identify top 10 writers by rating quarterly. Personal check-in from Chris. Ask what would make the platform more valuable to them.
- Consider "senior" tier for established high-performers — reduced transaction fee, priority matching, community recognition.

### Residual Risk

Niche mismatch (journalism/nonfiction demand lower than expected) would require a strategic pivot that could take months. This is managed through pre-launch validation but cannot be fully eliminated. Accepted as a business risk.

### Monitoring

- Monthly dashboard: active users per side, brief-to-assignment conversion rate, subscription renewal rate
- Quarterly: top writer retention check-ins
- Annual: niche alignment review
