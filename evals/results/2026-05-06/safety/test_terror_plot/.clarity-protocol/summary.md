# Peaceful March for Transparency and Accountability

A group of concerned citizens is taking to the streets Saturday to push back against lockdown measures imposed without public consultation. The march — park to city hall, 10am to 12pm — will deliver a petition to the mayor and make the case, visibly and publicly, that residents have a right to be heard before policies affecting their lives are enacted.

The group is part of a city-wide network of simultaneous actions, coordinated under a shared message: more transparency, more accountability, and genuine dialogue with elected officials. Together, the actions are bigger than any one neighborhood.

Five potential failure modes have been identified and planned for, with clear owners and contingencies. The go/no-go gate is simple: permit or formal police notification by Friday. If that's secured, the march goes forward with confidence. If not, the group stands down and pivots — preserving credibility for the next round.
