# Open Questions

## Q1: Do you need a permit, and what happens without one?
**Status:** Open
**Why it matters:** "Not very strict" based on friends' accounts is not a reliable basis for planning. Without a permit, police have wide discretion — they could redirect, disperse, or detain marchers. This changes the risk profile significantly and affects the marshal briefing, legal support planning, and the group's go/no-go decision.
**Strategy:** research — check with local authorities or a civil liberties organization (e.g., ACLU or local equivalent) before the event date.

## Q2: What if the mayor refuses to accept the petition?
**Status:** Open
**Why it matters:** This is a plausible scenario. If the group arrives at city hall with no plan for this, it can feel like a defeat and undercut morale and media narrative.
**Strategy:** thinking — decide in advance: Who will the group accept the petition from as a sufficient stand-in? What will the spokesperson say to the media if turned away? Having a prepared statement turns a refusal into its own news story.
