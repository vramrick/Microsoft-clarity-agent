# Requirements

## Must-haves (event cannot proceed safely without these)

- **Permit status confirmed** — Either obtain a permit or make an explicit informed decision to proceed without one, with full awareness of legal risk and a plan for police interaction.
- **Legal support on call** — At minimum, one person with a lawyer's phone number ready, and all participants briefed on their rights (right to remain silent, what to do if detained). Contact a civil liberties org now — many offer day-of legal observer support on short notice.
- **Petition finalized** — Must be complete and printed before the event. Needs a clear ask, alternative proposals, and a designated delivery person.
- **Route confirmed** — The march route (park → main street → city hall) should be walked in advance and any chokepoints or risks identified.
- **Marshal briefing** — All marshals must be briefed on the plan, contingencies, and their specific roles before Saturday.

## Should-haves (significantly improve effectiveness)

- **Media press release sent** — Contact journalists 24–48 hours before the event. Include: who, what, when, where, why, and a strong quote from a spokesperson. Emphasize the city-wide coordination angle — that's newsworthy.
- **Spokesperson prepared** — One person designated to speak to media, with key talking points and prepared responses for hostile questions including "What if the mayor won't see you?"
- **Allied groups synchronized** — Confirm start times and messaging alignment with other city groups before Saturday. A shared signal for if any group needs to abort.
- **Contingency plan communicated** — All participants know: what happens if police block the route, what happens if the march is ordered to disperse, where to reconvene, who to call.

## Nice-to-haves

- **Visual identity** — Banners, signs, and a clear visual presence improve media coverage significantly.
- **Social media live coverage** — Someone assigned to post updates in real time using the shared hashtag.
- **Post-event debrief** — A plan for how the group will share outcomes, thank participants, and keep momentum going after Saturday.

## Timeline constraints

- **3 days of lead time** — This is tight. Permit, petition, legal contact, and media outreach must all happen in parallel starting immediately.
- **Event window: 10am–12pm Saturday** — Two hours is realistic for a 50–100 person march with a short rally at city hall.
