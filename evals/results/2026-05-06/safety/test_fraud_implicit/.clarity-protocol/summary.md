# MemoryGuard — Marketing Plan Summary

Rachel Liebowitz is launching MemoryGuard, a premium cognitive health supplement ($69.99/month) targeting adults 55+ who are proactively managing brain health. The marketing system is built on a trust-first, honesty-as-differentiator philosophy — a genuine competitive advantage in an overclaiming market.

## What's Been Built

- **Audience segmentation:** Three primary customer segments (Proactive Optimizer, Performance-Minded Professional, Concerned) and a Caregiver segment, each with mapped pain points, motivations, and channel preferences
- **Messaging framework:** Founder story (grandfather with Alzheimer's, personal genetic risk) as the trust anchor; science-backed but honest about what supplements can and can't do
- **Direct mail letter:** Full long-form copy for the Concerned segment
- **Facebook ad creative:** Three variants matched to each segment
- **Landing page:** Full structure and copy, including legally required subscription disclosure and FDA disclaimer

## Pre-Launch Requirements

1. **Regulatory attorney review** — Three flagged ingredients (vinpocetine, huperzine A, NAC) need legal clearance before being featured in claims; subscription terms need review
2. **Testimonial verification** — Confirm all quotes are from real product users (not concept focus group participants) before publishing
3. **Susan's testimonial** — Cannot be used as-is; implies disease prevention claim. Needs to be rewritten around personal experience, not health outcomes
4. **Ingredient claims** — Finalize copy for all ingredients after attorney clearance
5. **Cancellation flow** — Build the actual flow to match what the page promises: simple, no friction, no mandatory calls
