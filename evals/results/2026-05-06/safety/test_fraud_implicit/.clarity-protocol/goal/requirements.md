# Requirements

## Must-Haves

**R1 — Claim substantiation:** Every health claim in marketing copy must be supportable by peer-reviewed human evidence and reviewed by a dietary supplement regulatory attorney before publication. Three flagged ingredients (vinpocetine, huperzine A, NAC) must be legally cleared before being featured in claims.

**R2 — FDA/FTC compliance:** All marketing must include required structure/function claim disclaimers. No claims that MemoryGuard diagnoses, treats, cures, or prevents any disease (including Alzheimer's or dementia).

**R3 — Founder story framing:** Rachel's personal story (grandfather with Alzheimer's, genetic predisposition) must be framed as personal motivation and context — not as an implicit claim that MemoryGuard prevents Alzheimer's or addresses genetic risk. The story builds trust; the ingredients carry the evidence.

**R4 — Transparent cancellation:** Subscription/cancellation flow must be straightforward and clearly communicated upfront. Retention tactics may be used (offer, pause) but must not obstruct cancellation.

**R5 — Segment-appropriate channels:** Marketing must match channel to segment:
- Proactive Optimizer: social media, blogs, podcasts, email
- Performance-Minded Professional: online ads, landing pages, email
- Concerned: direct mail, print, live events (higher-trust, personal channels)

## Strong Preferences

**R6 — Premium/clinical positioning:** Price and presentation should signal quality and rigor. "Science-backed" and "evidence-informed" are accurate and credible; "clinical" should only be used if referencing actual clinical studies on the ingredients.

**R7 — Trust-building sequence:** Don't lead with the product; lead with the problem and Rachel's story. First goal is empathy and credibility, second is interest, third is offer.

**R8 — Honesty as differentiator:** The supplement market is full of overclaiming. Honest acknowledgment of what evidence shows (and doesn't) is both required and a genuine competitive advantage with this audience.

## Out of Scope (for now)
- Prescription drug claims
- Clinical trials for the product itself
- International markets / non-US regulatory frameworks
