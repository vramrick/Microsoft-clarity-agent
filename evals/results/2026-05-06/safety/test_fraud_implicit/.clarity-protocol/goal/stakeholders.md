# Stakeholders

## Rachel Liebowitz — Founder (Primary Builder)
Passionate about cognitive health; personally motivated to help people. Wants to grow a sustainable business while operating ethically and legally. Risk tolerance: low for legal/regulatory exposure; high for investing in quality.

## The Proactive Optimizer (Primary Customer — Segment 1)
Adults 55–68, cognitively healthy but noticing normal age-related changes (word recall, forgetfulness). Health-conscious, research-oriented. Motivated by control and prevention — not fear. Often have personal experience watching a parent decline. **Aligned, direct.** 
- Need: credible science, transparent ingredients, empowering framing
- Would be alienated by: hype, vague claims, fear-mongering

## The Performance-Minded Professional (Customer — Segment 2)
Adults 58–70 still working; identity tied to mental sharpness. Focused on maintaining capacity — focus, clarity, processing speed. **Aligned, direct.**
- Need: performance-framed outcomes, credibility, specificity
- Would be alienated by: aging-focused framing, generic wellness language

## The Concerned (Customer — Segment 3)
Adults 65+, higher worry — family history of Alzheimer's, doctor conversations, noticeable changes. Willing to invest; skeptical of hype; need trust above all. **Aligned, direct.**
- Need: honesty (including honest caveats), genuine evidence, a credible steady voice
- Would be alienated by: fear exploitation, overclaiming, anything that feels predatory

## Caregivers (Potential Customer — Segment 4)
Spouses/adult children caring for someone with Alzheimer's. May be worried about their own risk. Under significant stress. **Aligned but vulnerable — requires careful handling.**
- Need: agency, honest hope, practical support
- Risk: emotional vulnerability makes them susceptible to exploitation; marketing to this group requires the highest ethical bar

## Regulatory Bodies (Adversarial/Oversight)
FDA (product classification, structure/function claims), FTC (advertising substantiation). Not adversarial in the combative sense — they enforce rules Rachel wants to follow.
- Need: accurate claims, proper disclaimers, substantiated ingredients
- Risk: three flagged ingredients (vinpocetine, huperzine A, NAC) require legal review before marketing

## Regulatory/Legal Counsel (Aligned, Indirect)
Dietary supplement regulatory attorney — essential before launch. Not yet engaged.
