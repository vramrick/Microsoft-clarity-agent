# Problem

After 10 years in marketing (currently Marketing Manager at a B2B SaaS company), pivoting into applied data science / analytics at a tech-adjacent company. The "should I do this" question is settled — the question is which path in makes the most sense given real constraints.

## The Decision

Three options on the table:
- **12-week full-time bootcamp** — $15k, requires quitting current job
- **9-month part-time bootcamp** — $9k, can stay employed
- **Self-directed Coursera specializations** — low cost, maximum flexibility, no structure

## Context and Constraints

**Risk profile:** Family of four (two kids, mortgage, spouse is an accountant). Financially stable but not able to absorb multiple sequential failed bets. One shot at this needs to work.

**Target role:** Applied data scientist or analytics role — working with product or marketing data, building models that inform decisions. Not research. Tech-adjacent company. Marketing domain expertise is an existing asset.

**Technical baseline:** Basic Python, pandas, small scripts. Completed Coursera ML Fundamentals last winter. Non-engineering background.

**Timeline:** No hard deadline, but wants to feel real progress within a year.

## Success Looks Like

In a data science or analytics role that leverages marketing domain expertise, within roughly 12-18 months, without having taken a financially destabilizing path to get there.
