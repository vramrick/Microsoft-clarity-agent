# Problem

A Director of Engineering is caught between a sales commitment and engineering capacity. VP Sales verbally committed a large enterprise customer to shipping SSO and provisioning features (SAML, SCIM, fine-grained roles, audit logging) by end of Q2. The deal is worth ~$1.2M ARR and the commitment is now embedded in the customer's procurement timeline.

The engineering team's most senior lead (Raj) says Q3 is the earliest realistic delivery date. His team is currently heads-down on a platform migration that the VP of Engineering prioritized last quarter. Slipping that migration creates downstream slippage on Data Retention and Growth Metering — internal but real commitments.

**The core tension:** Delivering the sales commitment requires breaking internal engineering commitments. Not delivering risks a $1.2M deal and damages sales/engineering trust. The decision is sitting at the Director level when it likely needs broader organizational alignment.

**Success looks like:** A clear path forward — whether that's a negotiated timeline, scoped delivery, or escalation — that the Director can defend to all parties with confidence.

**Organizational dynamics:** VP Sales knows there's tension. VP Engineering has deferred ("I'll back what you decide"). CEO is not yet briefed. The Director has been trying to hold this at their layer.
