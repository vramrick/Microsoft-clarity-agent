# Requirements

## Must-Haves

**R1 — Hypothesis-driven design**
The research must test the three leading hypotheses. Each must reach a clear verdict — confirmed, disconfirmed, or inconclusive — not just "we heard some things."

**R2 — Completable in 6 weeks with available resources**
Workstreams must fit within: Head of CS (interview-focused), 4 CSMs (supporting), shared analytics partner. No workstream should be a single point of failure.

**R3 — Segmentation output**
Findings must address whether churn skews by size band, vertical, or cohort — even if the answer is "no detectable pattern."

**R4 — Defensible to COO and board**
Findings must distinguish signal from noise. At minimum: quantitative data + qualitative corroboration, or a clear confidence level when only one source is available.

**R5 — Slide-ready output**
A 10–15 slide churn plan for annual planning. Potentially rolled up to board materials.

## Tiered Rigor Requirements

The three hypotheses map to different decision-makers and therefore require different levels of proof:

**Tier 1 — Headcount-ask quality (onboarding friction)**
This is the hypothesis where the Head of CS is asking for something (implementation specialist headcount or reallocation). The COO will push hard. Need: cohort data showing churn rate differential between accounts with/without dedicated implementation, corroborated by interview themes. Directional isn't enough — need a number.

**Tier 2 — Playbook-change quality (value realization gap)**
Actionable through CSM playbooks, coverage model, QBR redesign — no cross-functional ask required. Need: feature adoption analysis showing which capabilities churned accounts didn't use, corroborated by interviews. Clean enough to act on, not necessarily tight enough to defend to a skeptic.

**Tier 3 — Escalation quality (competitive pricing)**
The Head of CS can surface this but not decide the response. Need: enough evidence to raise credibly (CRM note frequency + interview mentions), not enough to defend a pricing decision. "We see it in X% of exits, here's what we heard" is sufficient.

## Strong Preferences

**P1 — Prioritize recent churns for interviews**
Accounts 3–6 months post-churn yield fresher recall. Interview outreach should lead with these.

**P2 — Don't self-report CRM tagging**
CSMs tagging their own accounts have conflicting incentives. Head of CS or analytics partner should do the tagging pass using a consistent schema. Cross-tagging (CSMs reviewing accounts they didn't own) is the fallback if additional context is needed.

**P3 — Don't overweight NPS**
15% response rate = heavy self-selection. Treat NPS verbatims as qualitative signal, not quantitative evidence.

**P4 — Onboarding and value realization may be the same story**
Accounts that didn't onboard well likely didn't realize value. The cohort analysis should test whether these are distinct causes or a single causal chain.
