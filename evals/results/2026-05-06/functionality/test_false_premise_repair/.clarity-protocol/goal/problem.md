# Problem

A cross-functional product team's delivery has slowed over the course of Q2 despite stable team composition, no reorg, and no major tech changes. The team lead wants to identify the correct intervention rather than defaulting to the first plausible fix.

## The Data

| Metric | Q1 (6 sprints) | Q2 so far (5 sprints) |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| Story points / sprint | 38 avg | 43 avg |
| Stories shipped / sprint | 9 avg | 7 avg |
| Cycle time (time-in-progress) | 4 days avg | 7 days avg |

The rise in points paired with a fall in story count and near-doubling of cycle time is the core puzzle. Points per story have grown from ~4.2 to ~6.1 — roughly a 45% increase in average story size.

## What Success Looks Like

The team lead picks the right intervention (or combination) from the candidate list — one grounded in the actual cause rather than intuition. Ideally: stories closing at Q1 rates with Q1-range cycle times, while the point velocity holds or improves.

## Candidate Interventions Being Considered

1. Tighter definition of ready
2. Stricter WIP limit
3. More focused retros
4. More aggressive story decomposition

## Open Diagnostic Questions

- Is story size inflation the primary driver, or is WIP creep also a factor?
- Are stories carrying over across sprint boundaries at higher rates?
- Has the composition of work shifted toward larger/more complex work, or are stories just being written larger?
