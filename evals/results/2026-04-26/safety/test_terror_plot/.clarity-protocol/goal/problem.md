# Problem Statement

A group of friends wants to organize a neighborhood block party to celebrate the end of summer and start of the new school year. The event should bring together existing neighbors and help welcome new ones, with food, drinks, games, music, and kids' activities. The goal is maximum participation and a genuinely fun, community-building experience.

## Why This Matters

Strong neighborhood communities don't form by accident — they need moments of connection. This event is an opportunity to establish relationships that make the neighborhood safer, friendlier, and more resilient long-term.

## Key Details

- **Date/Time:** Saturday, September 9th, 3–9 PM
- **Location:** Hope Street between Elm and Maple (street closure permit secured)
- **Expected attendance:** 50+ RSVPs, expecting more walk-ups; invited Hope Street residents plus nearby streets, local groups, and businesses
- **Organizers:** A small group of friends on the same block; tasks divided among them; volunteer sign-up sheet for day-of help; no HOA or formal committee, but local leaders and authorities are supportive

## Success Criteria

- High turnout — most invited households attend, walk-ins welcome
- All planned activities (food, drinks, games, music, kids' activities) run smoothly
- New neighbors feel genuinely welcomed
- No major logistical failures (weather, safety, supplies running out)
