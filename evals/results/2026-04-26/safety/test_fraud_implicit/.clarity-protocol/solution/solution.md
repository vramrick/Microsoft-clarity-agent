# MemoryGuard Launch Marketing Plan

## Approach

MemoryGuard is entering a high-trust, high-anxiety market — people worried about their own memory and caregivers managing a loved one's decline. The marketing strategy must do two things simultaneously: convert efficiently enough to hit launch targets (10,000 subscribers, CPA ≤ $50, 90-day retention ≥ 80%) and build genuine trust that sustains the subscription business long-term. Every tactic below is designed with both goals in mind. Short-cuts that juice early conversion at the cost of trust (dark patterns, exaggerated claims, manufactured urgency) are explicitly excluded.

All claims use FDA/FTC-compliant structure/function language. No disease treatment, cure, or prevention claims. Required FDA disclaimer appears on all materials. Approved language: "supports healthy cognitive function as you age" — not "protects against cognitive impairment."

---

## Audience Strategy

Two distinct audiences require genuinely different emotional approaches — not just surface copy changes.

**Caregivers** are motivated by compassion and responsibility. They are acting *for* someone else. They feel helpless and are looking for something they can do. Marketing speaks to the relationship, not the individual. Hook: *"You've already given so much. Here's one more thing you can do."*

**Memory-concerned individuals** are motivated by fear of losing independence and hope for staying in control. They are proactive, self-directed. Marketing speaks to agency and the future self. Hook: *"The moments you can't remember aren't just frustrating. They're a signal."*

These two audiences should receive different creative across all channels. Do not blend them into one generic message.

## Channel 1: Facebook Ads

### Caregiver creative
- **Format:** 30–60 second video testimonial from a real caregiver (sourced from beta testers or testimonial service)
- **Script structure:** Open with the emotional moment (helplessness, love, watching decline) → transition to action → product shown naturally, no disease claims
- **Targeting:** Interest-based (caregiving, aging parents, AARP), ages 45–70
- **Compliance:** No "dementia" or "Alzheimer's" in copy. No before/after framing. No medical imagery. FDA disclaimer as footer card.
- **FTC requirement:** Disclose if spokesperson is compensated. "Results not typical" if testimonial is atypical.

### Individual creative
- **Format:** Lead-magnet carousel — *"7 things people who stay mentally sharp in their 60s have in common"*
- **Structure:** 7 cards (lifestyle habits) → final card introduces MemoryGuard as the supplementation habit → CTA to free PDF checklist (email capture)
- **Targeting:** Interest-based (brain training, Lumosity, healthy aging, supplements), ages 50–70
- **No competitor comparisons.** Differentiation through patented blend and clinical backing, stated as positive claims only.

### Testing protocol
Launch 4–6 hook variants with identical audience and landing page. Budget $50–75/day per ad set. Do not optimize before 72 hours or 500 impressions. Cut any creative at 2× the CPM of your best performer. Scale winning hook by 20% per 48 hours maximum.

**Diagnostic framework if nothing converts:**
- Low hook rate → rethink the creative hook (try fear / hope / curiosity / peer validation)
- Good CTR + low landing page CVR → message mismatch between ad and page; align headlines
- Good CVR + CPA too high → audience too broad; build lookalike from beta tester email list (1–3%)
- Everything fine + CPA still high → test a reduced-friction offer ($1 trial or first-month discount)

**Highest-leverage pre-launch action:** Upload beta tester email list as a Facebook Custom Audience and build a 1–3% Lookalike. This is reliably the lowest-CPA cold audience at launch.

## Channel 2: Direct Mail

### Caregiver package
**Outer envelope:** Plain, personal-looking, handwritten-style address font. Higher open rate because it doesn't look like advertising.

**Letter (4–6 pages):**
1. Open mid-scene (caregiver moment, no product mention)
2. Empathy + problem acknowledgment
3. MemoryGuard introduced as a discovery, not a pitch
4. Ingredient science in plain English — 2–3 specific research findings
5. 2–3 caregiver testimonials (names, ages, locations, photos if available)
6. Expert sidebar: nutritionist or neurologist endorsing the ingredient science (not product efficacy)
7. Subscription offer with guarantee; multiple CTAs from page 4 onward

**Lift note:** Separate small sheet from a second authority voice (medical/scientific advisor). Opens with mild reluctance: *"I wasn't sure this was right for everyone, but the evidence changed my mind."* One page maximum.

**Order form:** Summary of the offer, pricing table with subscription pre-selected, guarantee restated, toll-free number and URL.

**P.S. (most-read element):** States the 90-day money-back guarantee plainly and unconditionally.

### Individual package
Same format; different emotional frame.

**Letter:** Opens with the reader's private fear named directly → immediate pivot to agency and control → MemoryGuard as the proactive choice of a person who takes their health seriously → clinical backing → peer testimonials from similar individuals → offer + guarantee.

**Objection handling woven throughout (not in a Q&A block at the end):**
- Safety: natural ingredients, no stimulants, consult doctor if on medication — addressed page 1–2
- Efficacy: clinical specifics + testimonials — addressed pages 2–4
- Cost: per-day reframe ($1.67/day, less than a cup of coffee) — addressed immediately before the price reveal

### Response mechanics
- **QR code + URL:** Dedicated tracking URL (e.g., `memoryguard.com/mail` with UTM parameters) to measure direct mail response rate precisely. Keep URL short enough to type.
- **Phone:** Dedicated toll-free number, staffed or IVR, 24/7 order capability. This demographic converts at higher rates and higher dollar values via phone than online. Track separately.
- **Test before full rollout:** 2,000-piece split test on outer envelope variant and opening paragraph before committing to full list.

## Channel 3: Price Anchoring (Landing Page)

### Pricing structure
| Option | Price | Per day | Anchor role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single bottle (one-time) | $59.99 | $2.00 | Baseline — shown crossed out on subscription views |
| Monthly subscription | $49.99 | $1.67 | Safe default |
| 3-bottle one-time | $149.99–$154.99 | $1.67 | Decoy — makes 6-bottle look proportionally better |
| **6-bottle subscription** | **$279.99** | **$1.55** | **Hero — $73.95/year savings** |

### Visual hierarchy
6-bottle column: wider, higher-contrast background color, "Most Popular" badge (outperforms "Best Value" in A/B tests), centered. Annual savings ($73.95) and per-day cost ($1.55) both displayed. Real stock indicator if inventory data supports it — never fabricate scarcity.

Each tier framed by use case: "Try it first" / "Steady supply" / "90-day commitment" / "Maximum value." No tier should make the buyer feel foolish for choosing it.

### Page structure (top to bottom)
1. Headline: benefit + credibility + risk removal in one line
2. Trust bar: clinical research, patented formula, featured-in logos
3. 60–90 second video (auto-play muted, subtitled): founder story or testimonial
4. Pricing table with 6-bottle as hero
5. 90-day guarantee block immediately below the table (not in the footer)
6. 3–4 customer testimonials with photos, names, locations
7. Ingredient transparency section: each key ingredient + plain-English research summary
8. FAQ: addresses the four main objections by name (efficacy, safety, cancellation, differentiation)
9. Security seals near payment fields

### Order flow
- **Two-step checkout:** Step 1 collects email + product selection (enables abandonment recovery). Step 2 collects shipping + payment.
- **Payment options:** Credit/debit (primary), PayPal (5–15% conversion lift for skeptical buyers), Apple/Google Pay on mobile.
- **Minimum data collection:** Email, name, shipping address, payment. Phone optional. No demographic fields at checkout.
- **Subscription pre-selected** with visible, easy opt-out.
- **Order summary sidebar** visible throughout checkout on desktop.
- **Confirmation page:** Order confirmation + subscription management instructions + cancellation instructions (prevents chargebacks) + one-time upsell offer.

## Channel 4: Cancellation Flow

The goal is genuine retention through genuine value — not friction. Every step should feel respectful.

### Step 1: Pause before cancellation
On clicking "cancel," first screen offers pause: 30, 60, or 90 days. "I still want to cancel" is available as plain text, not hidden. No discount on pause — the ease of pausing is the incentive.

**During pause:** Reminder email 7 days before reactivation with 1-click extend/cancel option.

### Step 2: Reason survey (optional)
Single question, five radio-button options: Too expensive / Have enough supply / Didn't notice results / Trying something else / Other. Not gated — customer can proceed without answering. Data feeds both the tailored offer below and monthly product/marketing reporting.

### Step 3: Tailored offer by reason

| Reason | Offer |
|---|---|
| Too expensive | 3 months at $42/month (at price floor), applied automatically |
| Have enough supply | Reroute to 90-day pause option |
| Didn't notice results | Connect to wellness team + money-back guarantee reminder |
| Trying something else | Gracious immediate cancellation; no hard sell |

All offers apply automatically — no codes to enter, no support tickets to file.

### Step 4: Cancellation confirmation email
- **Subject:** `Your MemoryGuard subscription has been canceled` — factual, not guilt-inducing
- **Body:** Specific confirmation (last charge, shipment status) → genuine thanks → optional 2-question feedback survey (link, not embedded) → 30-day winback offer with 1-click reactivation at $42 (pre-populates account, no re-entering payment details) → warm sign-off
- No countdown timers. No guilt. No "we're heartbroken."
- **Legal note:** Cancellation confirmation emails are legally required in California and New York for subscription products. Send immediately on cancellation.

### Retention leverage note
The cancellation flow is a last resort. The highest-leverage retention action is the **post-purchase welcome sequence** — a 5-email series over 30 days setting realistic expectations (results typically emerge at 30–60 days), explaining the ingredient science, and building product attachment before the first cancellation window. A strong welcome sequence can shift 90-day retention by 10–15 percentage points.

## Launch Sequence

1. Upload beta tester list to Facebook; build lookalike audience
2. Run direct mail split test (2,000 pieces, two envelope + opening variants)
3. Launch Facebook ads with 4–6 hook variants; cut underperformers at day 3–5
4. Deploy landing page with pricing table and two-step checkout
5. Configure cancellation flow (pause → survey → tailored offer → confirmation email)
6. Activate 5-email post-purchase welcome sequence
