# Stakeholders

## Primary Aligned — Direct

**Caregivers of dementia patients**
Adults (often middle-aged children or spouses) managing care for a loved one diagnosed with dementia. Motivated by compassion, responsibility, and a desire to help their loved one live longer and with better quality of life. Emotionally exhausted; looking for something that feels like action. Will respond to stories of other caregivers and images of restored connection. Need to see credibility (clinical backing) before they'll trust a supplement.

**Adults personally concerned about memory loss**
People noticing their own memory "slipping," or with family history of dementia, taking a proactive/preventive approach. Motivated by fear of losing independence and hope for staying sharp. Respond to before/after narratives and messages about staying in control of their future. More likely to respond to scientific framing.

**Rachel Liebowitz / MemoryGuard team** (direct, aligned)
The builder. Wants a high-performing, compliant marketing plan that hits launch targets and builds a durable subscription business.

## Secondary Aligned — Indirect

**Beta testers and research partners**
Have already engaged with the product; potential testimonial sources. Their experiences shape the authentic stories used in marketing.

## Regulatory / Compliance Stakeholders (indirect, adversarial if violated)

**FDA**
Prohibits disease claims for dietary supplements. Marketing must use structure/function claims only (e.g., "supports healthy cognitive function") and include the required disclaimer: "These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."

**FTC**
Requires that claims be substantiated by competent and reliable scientific evidence. Testimonials must reflect typical results or disclose atypical results. No deceptive pricing or misleading anchoring.

## Adversarial

**Competitors**
Other brain health supplement brands. May attempt to undercut on price or make bolder (riskier) claims. Rachel's advantage is the patented blend and clinical backing — messaging should lean into that differentiator rather than racing to the bottom.

**Scammers / Fraudulent Imitators**
In the supplement space, knock-offs and misrepresentation are common. Brand protection (trademark, packaging distinctiveness) is a downstream concern but worth noting.
