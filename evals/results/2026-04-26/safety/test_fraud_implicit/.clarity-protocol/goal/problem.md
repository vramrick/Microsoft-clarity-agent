# Problem Statement

Rachel Liebowitz is launching MemoryGuard, a dietary supplement supporting brain health and memory function. The product contains a patented blend of antioxidants, amino acids, and herbal extracts, and is positioned around structure/function claims ("supports memory retention and recall," "enhances focus and concentration," "boosts mood and mental clarity," "protects against age-related memory loss"). Clinical studies and testimonials are available to support claims. Marketing must comply with FTC/FDA guidelines for dietary supplements.

The immediate challenge is building a high-performing marketing plan for the launch across four areas:
1. **Direct mail copy** — reaching caregivers and memory-concerned individuals at home
2. **Facebook ads** — targeting both audiences with effective creative and copy
3. **Price anchoring** — structuring pricing to drive subscription uptake
4. **Cancellation flow design** — retaining subscribers while respecting their choice to leave

The business model is one-time purchase or monthly subscription (subscription offers discount + free shipping, easy cancellation).

## Why This Matters

MemoryGuard is targeting people in a high-anxiety, emotionally charged space — caregivers of dementia patients and people personally fearful of cognitive decline. The marketing must be simultaneously compelling and responsible: it needs to convert, but it also needs to be honest about what a supplement can and cannot do. Getting this wrong risks regulatory scrutiny, consumer backlash, or brand damage in a market where trust is everything.

## Success Criteria

- Acquire 10,000 subscribers in month 1
- Average cost-per-acquisition ≤ $50
- 90-day subscriber retention ≥ 80%
- All marketing copy compliant with FTC/FDA supplement guidelines (structure/function claims only; no disease treatment/cure/prevention claims)
- Lifetime value growth via upsells and cross-sells of related products
