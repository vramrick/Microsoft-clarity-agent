# MemoryGuard Launch Marketing Plan

## The Problem Worth Solving

Millions of Americans are quietly terrified about memory loss — either their own or a loved one's. Caregivers of people with dementia carry an enormous emotional load with few tools that feel like genuine help. People in their 50s and 60s noticing early forgetfulness are searching for something proactive to do. This is a real, urgent, underserved need.

MemoryGuard is a dietary supplement built to meet it — a patented blend of antioxidants, amino acids, and herbal extracts supported by clinical research, designed to support healthy brain function as people age. The product is credible. The market is ready. The launch challenge is reaching the right people with the right message, converting them into subscribers, and keeping them.

## The Approach

The plan targets two emotionally distinct audiences — caregivers acting out of love for someone else, and individuals acting out of hope for themselves — with tailored messaging across every channel. Generic "brain health" marketing talks to neither audience well. Specific, emotionally honest messaging earns trust in a category where trust is scarce.

Four channels work together:

- **Facebook ads** use real testimonial video for caregivers and a lead-magnet checklist carousel for individuals, with rigorous hook testing and a warm lookalike audience built from beta testers
- **Direct mail** delivers long-form letters (4–6 pages) with two distinct packages — caregiver version leading with relationship and compassion, individual version leading with agency and self-protection — supported by a lift note from a medical authority voice
- **Price anchoring** presents the 6-bottle subscription as the hero option through visual hierarchy and per-day framing ($1.55/day), using the 3-bottle as a decoy that makes the 6-bottle look like the obvious choice
- **Cancellation flow** leads with a genuine pause option, routes to tailored offers based on the cancellation reason, and closes with a gracious confirmation and 30-day winback — no dark patterns, no friction

All marketing uses FDA/FTC-compliant structure/function claims only. No disease treatment or prevention language.

## What Success Looks Like

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Subscribers acquired, month 1 | 10,000 |
| Average cost-per-acquisition | ≤ $50 |
| 90-day subscriber retention | ≥ 80% |

The 90-day retention target is the hardest and most important number. Most retention is won or lost in weeks 2–4 through the post-purchase welcome sequence — not the cancellation flow. A 5-email onboarding series that sets realistic expectations (results at 30–60 days), explains the ingredient science, and builds attachment to the product is the highest-leverage retention investment in the plan.

## The Risks That Matter Most

Eight failure modes are documented in detail. The two that require action before a single ad runs or a single letter mails:

1. **Regulatory (FM-3):** Legal review of all copy is non-negotiable. One uncaught disease claim can generate an FTC warning letter that requires pulling all materials. Get outside regulatory counsel to sign off before launch.

2. **Payment processor (FM-8):** Standard processors (Stripe, PayPal) routinely reject subscription supplement businesses. Establish a high-risk payment processor relationship and a backup before the checkout page goes live.

The other six risks — Facebook account suspension, high CPA, chargebacks, low retention, direct mail non-response, and audience backlash — each have documented prevention steps and mitigation plans. Most are manageable through the design choices already baked into the strategy (genuine easy cancellation, proactive billing reminders, split testing before full rollout).

## What Makes This Credible

The patented blend and clinical backing are the differentiators. Competitors making bolder claims without evidence are running a short-term strategy. MemoryGuard's positioning — honest, substantiated, built on real research — is a longer game that builds a brand worth defending. The marketing reflects that: every tactic is designed to attract customers who will stay, not just customers who will convert.
