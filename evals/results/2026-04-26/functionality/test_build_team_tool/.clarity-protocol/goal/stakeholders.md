# Stakeholders

## Team Members (the ~10-person engineering team)

**Type:** Aligned  
**Engagement:** Direct  
**Characteristics:** Engineers with high autonomy; work shifts frequently due to priorities, incidents, escalations. Mix of individual contributors and people with shared responsibilities (on-call, platform support). Varying habits around status communication — some consistent, some not.  
**Needs:** A frictionless way to share what they're working on; visibility into teammates' work to coordinate and avoid duplication; know who to ask for help.  
**Would be unhappy if:** The tool added overhead or process burden. Mandatory fields, rigid workflows, or frequent nagging would drive abandonment.

## VP / Team Leadership

**Type:** Aligned  
**Engagement:** Direct  
**Characteristics:** Non-technical or semi-technical; needs to answer "who's working on what?" to higher-ups. Wants self-service access — no dependency on someone else to pull a report.  
**Needs:** Real-time view of team activity; ability to generate and export/share a summary (link, copy-paste to slide or email).  
**Would be unhappy if:** The summary requires manual assembly, is stale, or isn't shareable outside the tool.

## Company-Wide Viewers

**Type:** Aligned  
**Engagement:** Direct (read-only)  
**Characteristics:** Other employees who may have an interest in or a stake in the team's work — other teams, cross-functional partners, curious colleagues.  
**Needs:** Visibility into what the engineering team is working on; knowing who to engage for input or collaboration.  
**Would be unhappy if:** The tool is locked down or hard to access.

## Adversarial / Misuse Actors

**Type:** Adversarial  
**Engagement:** Direct  
**Characteristics:** Low risk given internal-only audience. Unlikely threats: a disgruntled employee posting misleading status; someone using the open update field to post inappropriate content. Since this is company-wide, content visibility is real.  
**Concern:** If updates are entirely unmoderated and public within the company, someone could post something unprofessional or inaccurate. Worth considering a lightweight edit/delete capability.
