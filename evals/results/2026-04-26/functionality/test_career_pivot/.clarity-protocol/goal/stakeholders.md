# Stakeholders

## Primary

**The user (marketing manager, career pivoter)**
Direct stakeholder. Wants to transition into marketing data science while managing financial risk. Has 6 months runway but strongly prefers to stay employed. Cost-conscious but not cost-blocked. Needs structure and deadlines to learn effectively, but also values flexibility. Has domain expertise in marketing that is a genuine asset.

**Potential future employers**
Will assess her on: SQL proficiency, Python for data analysis, statistical reasoning, portfolio projects, and communication skills. Marketing domain expertise is a differentiator for marketing/growth/customer analytics roles, but would be neutral-to-irrelevant for general ML/research roles.

**Her current company's analytics team**
Indirect but high-value stakeholder. Works closely with marketing. She has an existing relationship with them. Could provide mentorship, project opportunities, or eventually a role. This is the highest-probability path to a first data role.

## Secondary

**Her current employer**
Has an interest in her productivity during any learning period. An internal transition benefits them (retains talent with combined marketing + data skills) and her (reduced risk, faster path, domain continuity).
