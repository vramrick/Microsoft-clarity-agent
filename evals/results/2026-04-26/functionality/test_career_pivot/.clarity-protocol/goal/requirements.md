# Requirements

## Must-haves (non-negotiable)

**R1 — Preserves employment income during transition**
She must not burn her runway prematurely. Any path must allow her to stay employed, or have a total cost (tuition + lost income + job search buffer) that fits within 6 months of savings. Traceable to: financial constraint + preference stated explicitly.

**R2 — Realistic for 10–15 hours/week**
If staying employed, the learning path must be completable at 10–15 hrs/week without requiring extended heroics. Traceable to: available time.

**R3 — Leads to a role she can actually get**
The target role must match what's achievable given her background — not an aspirational title that requires a CS degree or 3 years of ML experience. First role is likely Marketing Analyst, Customer Analytics Analyst, or Growth Analyst, not "Data Scientist." Traceable to: realistic career path for non-technical pivoters.

**R4 — Leverages her marketing domain expertise**
The path and target role should treat her 3+ years of marketing experience as an asset, not a liability. A pivot that discards her domain knowledge competes on weaker ground. Traceable to: competitive differentiation.

**R5 — Produces tangible portfolio signals**
Employers — especially for first roles — need proof of skills. The path must result in portfolio projects, not just certificates. Traceable to: hiring requirements for career changers.

## Strong preferences

**P1 — Cost below $15k total**
She's willing to invest but has other financial obligations. Full-time bootcamp ($15–20k tuition + 6 months lost income) exceeds this comfortably. Part-time bootcamp ($8–15k) or Coursera ($600–1,200/year) fits better.

**P2 — Structure with some flexibility**
Pure self-study has historically caused her to drift (finished courses but slower than expected). A path with deadlines, feedback, and community is preferred. Part-time bootcamp fits better than Coursera alone; Coursera augmented with external accountability is viable.

**P3 — Explore internal transition first**
Her current company's analytics team is a warm relationship. An internal move is faster, lower-risk, and preserves continuity. Any external plan should run in parallel with, not instead of, pursuing this.
