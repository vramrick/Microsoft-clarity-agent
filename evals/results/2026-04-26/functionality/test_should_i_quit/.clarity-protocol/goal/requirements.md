# Requirements

These are the criteria for evaluating job offers. Hard lines are non-negotiable. Quality signals are what make a role genuinely good vs. merely acceptable.

## Hard Lines (non-negotiable)

- **Compensation floor**: $120k minimum. $130k+ is happy territory; $150k is the target.
  - If an offer lands at $125k, negotiate before accepting — don't decline outright.
  - Currently earning ~$110k, so this is a meaningful step up.
- **Location**: Fully remote preferred. Hybrid acceptable only if role and team are genuinely strong. No relocation.
- **Product ethics**: No ads, gambling, surveillance, or weapons. Must be able to feel good about what's being built.

## Quality Signals (what makes a role genuinely good)

- **Product domain**: Positive signals — education, health, environment, social good. Neutral is tolerable; ethically questionable or boring is not.
- **Manager and team culture**:
  - Look for: clear communication, healthy feedback loops, constructive conflict resolution, psychological safety, genuine support for growth
  - Watch out for: controlling behavior, guilt-tripping, vague answers about team dynamics, pressure to always be available
  - Interview questions to probe: "What's something you wish you knew before joining?" / "How does the team handle disagreements?" / "Tell me about a failure and how it was handled."
- **Work-life balance**:
  - No Slack/email after 6pm or on weekends except genuine emergencies
  - Flexibility for appointments and personal time
  - No expectation of regular overtime or weekend work

## Gut Check

After evaluating against the above, ask: do I feel *excited*, *neutral*, or just *relieved*?
- Excited or neutral with strong signals: proceed
- Relief-only with weak signals: investigate further before accepting
