# Problem Statement

I've decided to leave my current job due to general dissatisfaction and want to execute the transition well. I'm in final rounds with one company and have two more interviews lined up for next week. I have a few months of savings but prefer not to use them. There's no specific urgency — I want to leave on good terms and land somewhere better.

The key decisions to work through:
1. How hard to push (or wait on) the current interview process vs. opening more pipeline
2. How to optimize the overall search — prioritization, pacing, leverage
3. Whether to give notice before or after securing an offer

## Why This Matters

A poorly timed or poorly managed exit can mean unnecessary financial stress, burning bridges, or accepting a suboptimal offer out of desperation. Getting the sequencing right maximizes both options and peace of mind.

## Success Criteria

- Land a role I'm genuinely excited about (not just "out of my current job")
- Don't dip into savings if avoidable
- Leave current employer on good terms
- Feel in control of the timeline rather than reactive
