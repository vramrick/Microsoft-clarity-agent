# Stakeholders

## Daniel (VP of Product) — primary user of this process
Needs a convincing AI roadmap and budget proposal. Success means walking into the board meeting with a defensible plan that shows strategic thinking, clear ROI, and realistic execution risk.

## The Board
Risk-averse, ROI-focused. Concerned about security and compliance risks from external AI. Will evaluate whether the AI strategy is credible and differentiated, not just reactive.

## The CEO
Driving the "AI story" pressure. Likely motivated by competitive positioning and investor narrative. Wants momentum.

## Customers (segmented)
- **Operationally frustrated:** Unhappy with OCR accuracy and miscategorization — want it to just work
- **Data-savvy / power users:** Want advanced analytics, automation, and customization
- **Prospects:** Some considering rivals with "AI-powered insights" — need a compelling near-term narrative

## Engineering Team (22 engineers, no ML experience)
Must build and maintain whatever gets committed to. Capacity and skill constraints are real. Some engineers are motivated to learn ML but need training and support.

## Data Scientists (elsewhere in company)
Potential internal resource. Not domain-familiar, currently occupied. Could be a bridge if engaged strategically.

## Competitors
Already partnering with AI companies or investing in ML teams. Ahead on messaging; unclear how much is shipped vs. marketed.
