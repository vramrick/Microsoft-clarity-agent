# Requirements

## For the Budget Meeting (end of April — days away)

1. **Investment case**: Clear articulation of why AI investment is necessary now, grounded in competitive pressure, customer feedback, and the cost of inaction
2. **Prioritized feature set**: Specific AI capabilities ranked by impact and feasibility, not a wish list
3. **Budget ask**: A range with justification — what does Year 1 cost, and what does it buy?
4. **ROI framing**: Quantified (even roughly) impact on churn risk, user productivity, and competitive positioning
5. **Risk acknowledgment**: Data privacy, team capability, and execution risk — and how they'll be managed

## For the Board Meeting (six weeks out)

6. **Phased roadmap**: At minimum three phases spanning 18 months — quick wins, intelligence layer, differentiation — with clear milestones
7. **Team plan**: How a 22-engineer team with no ML experience executes this — hiring, training, and internal partnerships
8. **Competitive differentiation narrative**: Why this company's AI will be better than what competitors can buy off the shelf
9. **Data strategy**: How the proprietary receipt corpus is an asset, not a liability

## Non-Negotiable Constraints

10. **Data privacy by design**: No approach that requires sending sensitive customer expense data to third-party AI providers without contractual/compliance controls
11. **Buildable by current team**: All features in Phase 1 must be achievable without ML expertise on the product team (ML-adjacent, not ML-dependent)
12. **Measurable outcomes**: Each initiative must have a success metric before it's funded
