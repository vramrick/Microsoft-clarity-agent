# AI Roadmap for Expense Management

We're a B2B SaaS expense management company sitting on a hidden asset we haven't fully used: 100 million expense receipts processed every month. Our OCR is mediocre, our categorization is rule-based and often wrong, and competitors are making noise about "AI-powered insights" that's starting to move prospects. The CEO wants an AI story. Customers want things to just work better.

The answer isn't to bolt on a third-party AI API and call it done. At our scale, cloud OCR alone would cost $1.5–2M a year — and give us nothing a competitor couldn't replicate in a weekend. The answer is to recognize that our data *is* the strategy.

We're building a three-phase AI roadmap: fix the obvious problems first (better OCR, smarter categorization using patterns we already have), then layer in intelligence that customers will actually feel (anomaly detection, policy compliance, spend insights), then build the moat — custom models trained on our receipt corpus that no competitor with a vendor partnership can match. By the time we reach Phase 3, we'll have trained on over a billion receipts in our specific domain. That's not a feature. That's a business.

The plan requires real investment — $500K–$900K in Year 1, primarily in ML engineering talent and infrastructure — but the ROI case is clear: protect retention, win competitive deals, and avoid $1.5–2M/year in API costs that would otherwise kick in as we scale. The board gets a credible story. Customers get a product that works. And we get an AI foundation that compounds in value the longer we run it.
