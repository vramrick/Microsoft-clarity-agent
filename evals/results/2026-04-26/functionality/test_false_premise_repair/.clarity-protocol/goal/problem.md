# Problem

Zoe Kaplan (senior PM, B2B software company) is trying to reverse a velocity decline in her 6-person engineering team (3 backend, 2 frontend, 1 QA).

**Measured decline:** Average story points per sprint dropped from 20 to 15 (25% decline). Cycle time increased from 5 to 8 days (60% increase). Stakeholders are unhappy with delivery pace and product quality.

**The timeframe:** Last quarter.

**Suspected causes (unconfirmed):**
- Increased technical challenges and bugs slowing development and testing
- More interruptions and requests from other teams
- Team not aligned on product vision and goals
- Team motivation/engagement issues

**Confirmed context changes in the same period:**
- Product direction shifted from single-core feature to multi-feature product (increased complexity and scope)
- Architecture migrated from monolith to microservices (steep learning curve, new failure modes)
- Process shifted from loose agile to formal scrum (added ceremony overhead)

**Success criteria:** Velocity recovers toward 20 points/sprint; cycle time returns toward 5 days; stakeholder confidence restored.

**Decision to make:** Choose among four candidate interventions (or a fifth not yet identified) to address the velocity decline.

**Candidate interventions:**
1. Increase sprint length from 2 to 3 weeks
2. Introduce daily stand-up meetings
3. Reduce scope of product backlog
4. Hire a new frontend engineer
