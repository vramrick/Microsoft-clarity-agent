# Problem

## What we're solving

An engineering organization of 30 has outgrown its 3-team structure. The current structure creates a Platform bottleneck (Growth and Data both depend on it), a scope problem (Growth covers two distinct workstreams with different tech stacks), and a leadership gap (Data's EM is 4 months in and under-supported for a team of 10).

## The proposal under consideration

Reorganize from 3 teams to 5:

- **Split Growth (12)** → Acquisition (6) + Retention (6), each with a tech lead promoted from within
- **Split Platform (8)** → Foundations (5) + Developer Experience (3). Foundations reports to the EM (the author). DX reports to Maya, whose role is reframed as "principal engineer with a small team" rather than manager.
- **Add an EM under Lina** in Data (10), to support her growth toward director.
- Net effect: 3 teams → 5; direct reports to author: 3 → 5.

## What the author has already done

- Skip-level 1:1s with 12 engineers to validate bottleneck and scope concerns
- Three drafts of this proposal
- Informal read-out to CTO (supportive, wants pressure-testing)
- Considered and rejected: matrix model, functional split, fixing with process

## What hasn't happened yet

- The three current leads (Maya, Darius, Lina) have not been told
- Communication sequencing not yet decided

## What we're pressure-testing

1. Risks not yet identified
2. Whether the Platform split actually resolves the bottleneck
3. Whether Maya will accept the principal-engineer framing or read it as demotion
4. Whether adding an EM under Lina sets her up to grow or undercuts her
5. How to sequence communications to leads, engineers, and company
