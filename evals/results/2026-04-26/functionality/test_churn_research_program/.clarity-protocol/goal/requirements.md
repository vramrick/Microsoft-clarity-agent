# Requirements

## Research program requirements

### Must do
- Produce a diagnosis of churn root causes with sufficient confidence for an executive presentation (well-reasoned and evidence-based, with limitations acknowledged)
- Focus analysis on highest-impact segments: enterprise accounts, high-value product tier, recent cohorts
- Triangulate findings across at least three data sources to reduce single-source bias
- Involve marketing and product as active contributors (not just reviewers) at defined points in the process
- Deliver final findings and recommendations within six weeks
- Operate within a team of three, using free or low-cost tools

### Must produce
- A diagnosis: the most likely and impactful drivers of churn, with evidence and confidence levels
- Actionable recommendations: specific, feasible interventions for retention and growth
- An executive presentation: structured for a cross-functional leadership audience
- A research summary: methodology, data sources, limitations, and assumptions documented

### Should do
- Prioritize qualitative methods (interviews, exit analysis) given data quality constraints
- Standardize interview and analysis approaches so findings are defensible and replicable
- Surface cross-functional implications for marketing (messaging/positioning) and product (features/integrations)
- Distinguish between the gradual year-long churn pattern and the sharp recent drop-off — they may have different causes

### Constraints
- Six-week deadline is fixed
- Team of three (customer success background, some data/research experience)
- Limited budget — free or low-cost tools only
- Data anonymization required
- Data access limitations: product analytics are hard to export; exit interviews are unstandardized; NPS response rates are low

## What success looks like

Elena walks into the executive presentation with:
1. A clear story about why customers are leaving (the top 2–3 drivers, with evidence)
2. A credible action plan with prioritized recommendations
3. Marketing and product already aligned on their roles in the response
4. An honest account of what we know, what we're less sure about, and what we'd need to learn more
