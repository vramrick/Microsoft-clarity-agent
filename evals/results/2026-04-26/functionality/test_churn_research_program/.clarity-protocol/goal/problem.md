# Problem

## What's happening

A B2B SaaS company (productivity and collaboration software) lost approximately 200 of ~1,000 customers over the past year — a ~20% annual churn rate, well above healthy B2B SaaS norms. The current active base is ~800. Churn includes both a sharp drop-off last quarter and a steady year-long drift. No clear pattern or trigger has been identified.

## Why it matters

Churn at this rate threatens revenue, growth, and the company's ability to invest in the product. The COO has requested both a diagnosis and an action plan, to be presented to the executive team in six weeks.

## The core challenge

Elena (Head of Customer Success) and a team of three must design and execute a research program that:
1. Diagnoses root causes of churn using fragmented, inconsistent data across five sources (product analytics, NPS/CSAT, exit interviews, support tickets, CRM)
2. Focuses on the highest-impact segments: enterprise accounts, high-value product tier, recent cohorts
3. Produces actionable retention and growth recommendations
4. Involves marketing and product as active contributors
5. Delivers findings to the executive team at the six-week mark

## Working hypothesis

The company may be failing to deliver on its value proposition — not demonstrating how the product drives productivity and collaboration, not clearly differentiating from alternatives, and possibly missing key features or integrations.

## Data landscape

Five sources available, all with limitations:
- **Product usage analytics** — proprietary system, difficult to export
- **NPS/CSAT** — low response rates, not segmented by customer type
- **Exit interviews** — unstandardized, dependent on account manager availability
- **Support tickets** — available but not systematically analyzed for churn signals
- **CRM data** — exists but not up to date

## Constraints

- 6-week deadline; small team of 3 (all customer success background, some data/research experience)
- Limited budget (free/low-cost tools preferred)
- No legal/privacy blockers — anonymization and aggregation sufficient
- Some access to both loyal active customers and churned customers willing to share feedback

## Success criteria

- Evidence-based diagnosis of churn root causes, with confidence sufficient for executive presentation
- Specific recommendations for retention and growth
- Cross-functional alignment with marketing and product
- Executive team presentation ready at six weeks
