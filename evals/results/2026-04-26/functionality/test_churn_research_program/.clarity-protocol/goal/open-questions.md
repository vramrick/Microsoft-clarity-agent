# Open Questions

## Q1: What's causing the sharp drop-off last quarter?
**Status:** open
**Why it matters:** The recent spike may have a distinct cause from the gradual drift — a product change, a pricing change, a competitor move, or a macro event. If so, it deserves separate investigation and may yield faster wins.
**Strategy:** research
**Approach:** Cross-reference the timing of the Q4 drop-off against product releases, pricing changes, competitive moves, and support ticket volume spikes.

## Q2: How reliable and representative are the existing exit interviews?
**Status:** open
**Why it matters:** Exit interviews are the richest qualitative signal available, but they were unstandardized and may reflect account manager bias or selection effects. Before treating them as evidence, we need to know how much to trust them.
**Strategy:** research
**Approach:** Audit the existing interviews: who conducted them, for which accounts, using what questions, and what patterns emerge. Assess coverage gaps by segment.

## Q3: Is low product usage a leading indicator of churn in our customer base?
**Status:** open
**Why it matters:** If usage data is a reliable predictor, it enables proactive intervention — even with the export constraints. If it's not, the team shouldn't over-invest in the analytics data.
**Strategy:** research
**Approach:** Pull whatever usage data is accessible and compare engagement patterns between churned and retained accounts, especially in the high-value and enterprise segments.

## Q4: What do churned customers in the priority segments actually say drove the decision?
**Status:** open
**Why it matters:** The working hypothesis (value prop delivery failure, feature gaps) is plausible but untested. Direct customer voice is the most credible evidence for an executive audience.
**Strategy:** research
**Approach:** Conduct standardized retrospective interviews with a sample of churned customers from enterprise and high-value tiers willing to participate.

## Q5: What are loyal customers getting from the product that churned customers weren't?
**Status:** open
**Why it matters:** Contrast between churned and retained customers helps isolate causation from correlation, and surfaces what the product does well — important for both retention and the narrative.
**Strategy:** research
**Approach:** Interview or survey a matched sample of active customers in the same segments to identify differentiating factors.
