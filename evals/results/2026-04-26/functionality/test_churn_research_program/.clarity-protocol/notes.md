# Notes

## Guiding principles

- This is a time-boxed research program (6 weeks, team of 3), not an open-ended investigation. Every decision should be evaluated against the deadline.
- Qualitative methods are primary given data quality constraints. Don't over-invest in fixing the quantitative picture.
- The program has a hard political dimension — marketing and product must be co-owners, not subjects. COO sponsorship is essential.
- Transparency about limitations builds credibility with the exec audience. Acknowledge gaps; don't hide them.

## Key observations

- 20% annual churn is significantly above B2B SaaS norms. The urgency is real and the exec team will be watching closely.
- The Q4 spike and the year-long drift may have different causes. Keep them analytically separate throughout.
- Week 1 is the highest-leverage week. Four of six identified failure modes have their best prevention points there: launch interview outreach, test analytics access, schedule workshop blocks, agree scope-change process with COO.
- The cascade chain to watch: execution breakdown → analytical error → cross-functional resistance → no action. Preventing execution breakdown is the single highest-leverage intervention.
- Elena's working hypothesis (value prop delivery failure) is plausible but untested. The program should actively look for competing explanations.
