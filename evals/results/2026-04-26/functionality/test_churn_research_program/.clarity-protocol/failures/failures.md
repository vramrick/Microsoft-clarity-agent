# Failure Modes

1. **[Data Collection Bias](failure-01-data-collection-bias.md)** (High) Interview data doesn't accurately represent reality due to social desirability bias, unrepresentative samples, interviewer influence, or fatigue. The team analyzes data that looks valid but misrepresents why customers actually left. Diagnosis points to the wrong drivers. **No mitigation plan yet.**

2. **[Analytical Error](failure-02-analytical-error.md)** (High) The team collects valid data but reasons about it incorrectly — confirming the starting hypothesis rather than testing it, applying inconsistent coding, conflating the Q4 spike with the year-long drift, or failing to reconcile conflicting signals. Findings are internally consistent but wrong. **No mitigation plan yet.**

3. **[Data Access Failure](failure-03-data-access-failure.md)** (Medium) Key data sources are blocked — product analytics can't be exported, or account managers withhold exit interview notes. The team proceeds with a thinner evidence base than planned. Credibility risk rises if gaps aren't disclosed. **No mitigation plan yet.**

4. **[Program Execution Breakdown](failure-04-program-execution-breakdown.md)** (High) The timeline collapses: data audit overruns, interview recruitment is slow, workshops slip, or the COO redirects scope mid-program. Synthesis and presentation are rushed. The deliverable is incomplete or misaligned with expectations. **No mitigation plan yet.**

5. **[Cross-Functional Resistance](failure-05-cross-functional-resistance.md)** (High) Marketing or product reject, deflect, or soften findings that implicate their work. Internal political dynamics prevent honest cross-functional engagement. Findings are diluted before the executive presentation. Remediation plans lack genuine ownership. **No mitigation plan yet.**

6. **[Post-Program Failure](failure-06-post-program-failure.md)** (High) The program succeeds technically but produces no lasting change — no resource commitments, no measurement framework, or external factors render recommendations stale. Churn continues. The research program is wasted. **No mitigation plan yet.**

## Cross-Cutting Patterns

**Pinch point: Early engagement and framing.** Failures 1, 4, and 5 all have their most effective intervention points in week 1. Bias safeguards, interview recruitment, workshop scheduling, COO sponsorship framing, and scope change agreements should all be addressed before substantive work begins. A strong week-1 setup prevents the majority of cascading risks.

**Pinch point: Explicit acknowledgment of limitations.** Failures 1, 2, 3, and 6 all benefit from the same intervention: be explicit about what you don't know, what data you couldn't access, and where confidence is low. Transparency about limitations increases credibility rather than reducing it, and prevents findings from being challenged post-presentation.

**Cascade chain.** The most dangerous path: Failure 4 (execution breakdown) compresses the synthesis phase → Failure 2 (analytical error) is more likely under time pressure → Failure 5 (cross-functional resistance) is harder to navigate when findings are less well-evidenced → Failure 6 (post-program failure) is the outcome. Preventing Failure 4 is the highest-leverage single intervention.
